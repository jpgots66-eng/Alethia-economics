Revenue Simulator v5 · Alethia Team LLC · June 2026
How this model works. Three layers build on each other. Layer 1 is the crew ceiling — the hard physical limit of how much revenue the technicians can produce. Layer 2 is sales activation — what happens when Connor is freed from field work to focus on selling. Layer 3 is the revenue stack — adjacent services, partnerships, Christmas lights, and proximity that push past the crew ceiling without adding crew hours. The freedom rate slider controls how much of Connor's time goes to sales versus operations. As that number rises, so do the staffing costs required to replace him in those operational roles.
SDE — Seller's Discretionary Earnings
The total economic benefit the business produces for one owner-operator. Net profit plus owner salary plus personal expenses through the business. This is the number a buyer uses to value the company. At a 3–5x multiple it sets the exit price. Adding staff increases revenue but reduces SDE in the short term — the model shows that trade-off honestly.
Dynamic Customer Pool
Every year the one-time customer database grows from new job acquisitions and shrinks by plan conversions. The reactivation campaign works this pool. By Year 3 the pool is 600–700 customers — nearly three times today's base — making the reactivation lever more powerful every year, not less.
Connor's Freedom Rate
Currently Connor spends roughly 30% of his time on sales and 70% on field oversight, admin, and operations. As the freedom rate rises toward 100%, estimate volume grows proportionally. But each 10% of freedom requires staffing investment — field supervisor, admin coordinator — that comes out of SDE before revenue recovers it.
Crew Ceiling
One crew at full capacity generates roughly $220K in window cleaning revenue. Two crews generate roughly $440K. This ceiling does not move without stable technicians. Every other growth lever operates above this floor — plan members, adjacent services, partnerships, and Christmas lights are what push total revenue past what the crew can physically produce in a day.
